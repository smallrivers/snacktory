Muskogee Roughers football team collecting donations for Jenks Trojans assistant coach Bryant Calip

Liz Bryant
7:54 AM, Oct 21, 2014
3:06 PM, Oct 28, 2014

MUSKOGEE, Okla. - One hundred sixty feet.

That's the distance that separates two opposing football teams, but for the Muskogee Roughers freshman team there is only one side of the field when it comes to fighting an illness.

"Even though we're competitors, coaching is kind of a fraternity," said 9th grade Muskogee head coach Don Yates. "When you see someone in need, you step out and try to help."

The team is collecting donations for Jenks Trojans assistant football coach Bryant Calip, who is battling a rare form of blood cancer. Calip coaches cornerbacks for the Trojans' varsity team.

In August, Calip was diagnosed with Lambda Multiple Myeloma and is currently undergoing treatment in Tulsa. According to the Go Fund Me site set up to help Calip and his family offset the hospital bills, he is expecting to complete his treatment in January.

"You just learn, don't give up. You fight until the end. It's about like football, four quarters, fight until the end, until the clock is gone," Calip said.

Jenks Junior Dillon Stoner said Coach Calip doesn't look sick nor act sick despite undergoing blood transfusions and now three rounds of chemotherapy.

"He just never slows down, which I think is most amazing about him," Stoner said.

Calip's twin brother Brad Calip, who is also a Jenks football coach, says he senses his brother's pain.

"I told him 'this is pretty scary for you.' He said 'yeah.' I saw the tears in his eyes, tears in my eyes. I told him I loved him then. It was pretty hard, pretty tough."

The Muskogee freshmen are collecting donations at the following home games:

  • Oct. 21 The 8th grade team at 6:30 p.m.
  • Oct. 24 The varsity game at 7 p.m. against Sand Springs
  • Oct. 28 The team will hand the money over during the Muskogee at Jenks game.

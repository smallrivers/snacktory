      Wife of Atlanta firefighter who died during training ride for New Orleans Ironman spoke at race Sunday

      KimGuinn.jpg
      Kimberly Guinn, wife of Atlanta firefighter Frank Guinn who was killed in New Orleans while training on Friday, speaks to the participants of Sunday's Ochsner Ironman 70.3 New Orleans prior to the race. (Andrew Lopez, NOLA.com | The Times-Picayune)
      Print
      Andrew Lopez, NOLA.com | The Times-Picayune By Andrew Lopez, NOLA.com | The Times-Picayune The Times-Picayune
      Email the author | Follow on Twitter
      on April 13, 2014 at 4:08 PM, updated April 13, 2014 at 5:42 PM
      View/Post Comments

      Kimberly Guinn took the microphone prior to Sunday's Ochsner Ironman 70.3 New Orleans to address the athletes assembled.

      Her husband was supposed to be one of those athletes. But Atlanta firefighter Sgt. Frank Guinn was killed Friday when a vehicle struck him as he rode a bike on the race course Friday morning.

      She successfully fought back tears, because as she said, that's what Frank would have wanted her to do.

      Guinn said Frank's brother-in-law Andrew Powell, who remains hospitalized with injuries suffered during the crash, continually asked Kimberly Guinn and his wife Sandra what day it was. When they answered, he said how many days they were from the race -- first two days, then one day.

      Premier Event Management race director Bill Burke, who fought back tears when he spoke after Guinn on Sunday morning, said the triathlon community has helped to rally around the Guinn family, who had 7-year old triplet girls.

      "When a guy travels to your race from another city and tragically loses his life, the first thing I thought of was of how to say how much the triathlon community loves this guy," Burke said. "At the end of the day, when the race is over and this is gone, she has to raise those kids. I spoke with a family friend in Salt Lake City (of the Guinns) and they've started a fundraising campaign.

      "We reached out to all of our athletes and sent an email this afternoon to 73,000 athletes in our database. The theme of the email was 'Help us, help them.' We're hopeful that athletes and triathletes and people in general will see the need and the importance to open their pocket books at a time like this and make a small donation. It's the least we could do."

      As of Sunday afternoon, a gofundme.com account set up for the family raised over $25,000, and one for Powell had raised over $8,000.

      Ochsner Ironman 70.3 New Orleans winner Andy Potts and Kimberly Guinn after the race.Andrew Lopez, NOLA.com | The Times-Picayune

       Burke said he was amazed by the strength that Kimberly Guinn showed throughout the day. After speaking to all of the triathletes prior to the race, Guinn was also at the finish line to greet men's winner Andy Potts when he finished the race.

      "Kimberly was a real strong woman today," Burke said. "She came to the race. She wanted to be here. She told me throughout the morning that she was supposed to be here. We gave her his finisher's cap, his medal and his race number. We did the same thing for Andrew, the other gentleman that's still in the hospital." 

      In the races, former world champion Andy Potts won the men's division and Lauren Barnett made a late surge to win the women's division.

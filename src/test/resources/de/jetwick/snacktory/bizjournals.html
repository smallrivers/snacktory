Rackspace plans 500 new jobs at Highland Mall, seeks incentives

Rackspace Inc., the San Antonio-based Web hosting company that plans to ramp up an already significant Austin presence, is seeking a state incentives package worth $1.25 million in tax rebates, according to documents filed with the Travis County Commissioners Court.

In July, the company announced that it would move its operations to new offices at the Austin Community College's campus at the former Highland Mall, bringing with it more than 600 employees currently working in Travis County. The company plans to open its 185,000-square-foot office in what was a Dillard's department store in late 2015.

In its filing, Rackspace (Nasdaq: RAX) highlights the ongoing transformation of the former Highland Mall area, and the possible expansion of nearby rail public transportation, as a recruiting opportunity.

"Current light rail and future urban rail provide opportunities to attract talent from technical, talent-rich areas in the downtown core and the northern suburban areas through public transit and reduced commutes," reads a company memo filed with the Travis County Commissioners court. "Rackspace will be a part of the revitalization of a once thriving, now depleted area of Central Austin."

Tuesday, Rackspace's expansion will go before the Travis County Commissioners Court, which will consider nominating the company's local expansion as a Texas Enterprise Zone project. Click here to see how much the program offers to qualifying companies adding jobs.

If it gets the approval of Travis County elected officials, the project would be able to apply for incentives that would allow it to seek as much as $1.25 million in sales tax rebates. As a state Enterprise Zone program, the county would not be expected to offer any additional incentives.

In the filing with the Commissioners Court, Rackspace said it plans to invest $32 million in its new facility. As part of its expansion, the company said it plans to add an additional 500 jobs over the next decade at an average wage of $88,600. Those jobs break down across 100 entry level jobs, 320 staff jobs, 40 supervisors, 20 managers and 20 executive positions.

The filing reveals that the company plans to spend $10.6 million on construction labor, $8.7 million on building renovation costs, $3.5 million on equipment, and $9.4 million on furniture and other fixtures.

Michael Theis covers local government, courts and economic development for the Austin Business Journal.

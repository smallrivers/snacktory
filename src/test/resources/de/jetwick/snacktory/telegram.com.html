  • SHREWSBURY — For a time, Tuesday night’s matchup between the Lincoln-Sudbury and St. John’s basketball teams had the makings of a competitive game.
    And then, after a first quarter in which the long ball had served them so well, the Warriors’ shooting went dry. And when St. John’s brought the game inside, the distance between the teams became glaring.
    Nick Lukasevicz had 24 points and nine rebounds, Sean Burke had 12 points and 12 rebounds, and St. John’s used a dominant performance in the paint to pull away in the second half and defeat the Warriors 90-70 on their home court in a non-league tilt.
    St. John’s improved to 12-1 while the Warriors fell to 5-5.
    “We did hit the boards,” said St. John’s coach Bob Foley. “They gave us a run early in that first quarter. They were ahead the whole time, but all of a sudden, these kids, once they start running we have a pretty good team.”
    Indeed, L-S seemed every bit the equal of the Pioneers for the first eight minutes of the game, taking a slim 20-17 lead into the second quarter.
    The Warriors kept momentum going thanks to a strong first-half performance from Jamal Allen, who scored 16 of his 19 points in the first two quarters, including a stretch late in the second quarter where he hit three 3-pointers, one of which tied the game 29-29 with 3:30 remaining in the first half.
    And then the Pioneers settled down, with Burke going coast-to-coast after a vicious block on Allen, sending St. John’s on a 15-8 run through the end of the half.
    Lukasevic caught fire, scoring the last eight points in the stretch including a 3-pointer at the buzzer that sent the Pioneers into the half with a 44-37 advantage.
    “We started off a little slow,” said Lukasevic. “We have a young team so we started a little slow, but we really came out strong in the second quarter and that allowed us to get some fast break points, knocked down some 3-point shots which gave us some momentum.”
    Indeed, halftime did little to slow the St. John’s roll.
    The Pioneers came out of the break hot, opening the second half with a 10-4 run, capped by a put-back from Lukasevic that forced L-S to take a timeout.
    Lukasevic kept the hot hand for St. John’s, scoring 10 points in the third quarter while his team used a suffocating defense to build a commanding lead.
    Joe Murphy came off the bench and played well, scoring six points in the quarter including a layup with 1:48 remaining in the third, drawing a foul and hitting the free throw to give St. John’s a 66-49 lead.
    Lukasevic ended the quarter much the same way he did the previous one, with a basket at the buzzer that put St. John’s up 73-51 heading into the final eight minutes of play.
    “What you get out of (Lukasevic) is offensive rebounds, and he can put them in, but really he’s a 3-shooter,” said Foley. “He’s a power forward but very versatile and he had a great game for us.”
    St. John’s outscored L-S 29-15 in the quarter, dominating the boards and making the most of its second chances while shutting down the Warriors’ long game.
    Foley was quick to commend the play of his point guard, Alex Bradley (10 points, seven assists, four rebounds), who shut down Allen in the second half.
    “That kid (Allen) is a very good player and really hurt is in the first half,” said Foley. “My kid is only a sophomore, and I thought just really played great defense and took that kid out of the game.”
    With the result largely decided heading into the final quarter, each team turned to its reserves to play out the final minutes.
    Murphy had four points in the final quarter for St. John’s while Nick Grizon came off the bench to score five points for L-S, including a layup that turned into a three-point play, bringing the score to 84-64 with just over two minutes remaining.

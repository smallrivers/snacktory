      Crowdfunding trend growing for cancer patients

      Jade DeGood, jdegood@kwch.com
      POSTED: 07:41 PM CDT Jun 14, 2015   UPDATED: 11:32 PM CDT Jun 14, 2015
      • Small Text
      • Medium Text
      • Large Text
      HUTCHINSON, Kan. -

      For 5-year-old Prestun Siebel being a kid hasn't been easy.

      More from KWCH.com

      • Superboy fighting cancer celebrates birthday
      • People gather to support boy battling cancer

      "Prestun is stage four Neuroblastoma," said his mom Tiffany of Hutchinson. "He has been fighting cancer almost three years. He was cancer free for 18 months and then in July of last year he relapsed in the brain."

      Eyewitness News first met the Siebel family when they were in New York City for Prestun's treatment. You can view that story here.

      Prestun and him mom spent the last 10 months in a New York City hospital.

      "As a parent you do whatever you have to do that's necessary and don't worry about the rest until it all comes into the mailbox," said Tiffany.

      The medical bills continue to roll in.

      "We just received a medical bill for his proton radiation and it was for a total of $180,000," said Tiffany. "After insurance paid, it was down to $139,000. We are responsible for the remaining balance because we are out of the network. Proton therapy is not considered conventional treatment, it's still considered experimental, so insurance is not really accepting this form of treatment. Since Prestun had cancer in his brain, the team in New York thought proton treatment was the best and safet option for him."

      That is just one bill from three years of treatment, so Prestun's family has turned to others online for help.

      "We've raised probably about $55,000 in all, with donations and the Go Fund Me site," said Tiffany. "Without these people, I truly believe my son wouldn't be alive.

      Each year more people turn to crowdfunding sites like Go Fund Me to help pay for medical expenses.

      From 2011-2014 campaigns that specifically mention "cancer" have raised over $205-million across more than 106,000 campaigns.

      "It's hard for us to ask for help, but when you are dealing with a non-curable cancer and you know his best chances of hope is New York City you just have to ask for help," Tiffany said. "My hear is filled with love. I'm so grateful for people who care about Prestun. It's unbelievable the kindness we've received throughout this transition of the entire three years."

      Tiffany said although the bills continue to add up, the treatment seems to be working.

      "He's currently clear, no evidence of disease right now so the treatment is working," she said. "Now we just have to pray to the man upstairs that he continues to stay clear."

      Prestun's family said they'll take cancer-free over debt-free any day.

      "We still have a long ways to go, we still have at least another two years where every three months minimum we have to go to New York City for scans or more treatment," she said. "But that's the best news ever, that your son is clear."

      It is important to mention that not all of the funds collected on sites like Go Fund Me go directly to the person in need. Go Fund Me collects about 8% of the donations. Plus, if donations total up to a certain amount, that money is taxed.

      To donate to Prestun's Go Fund Me page click here or you can send it through the mail to P.O Box 1925, Hutchinson, KS 67504. Prestun also loves super heroes if you would like to send him some positive thoughts in a card.

      If you would like to follow Prestun's journey click here to go to his webpage.

      NOTE from Go Fund Me: Donors should only contribute payments to GoFundMe users they personally know and trust. We’re often asked how a donor can tell the authenticity of a personal cause found on GoFundMe. Unfortunately there is no way to 100% guarantee that a user’s GoFundMe donation page contains accurate or truthful information. As such, donors should not make payments to any campaigns or people unless they fully understand and trust the cause presented.
